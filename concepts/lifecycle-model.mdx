Every contract moves through a predictable set of stages, from the day it's signed to the day it ends. HarborOS tracks the contract by its stage in that life — not by the emails, tasks, and meetings happening around it. The stage is what determines how a contract counts toward your numbers.

## The stages

A contract moves through these stages in order:

- **New** — Signed and starting. Its [ARR](/get-started/glossary) joins your active base as new business.
- **Active** — Live and being delivered. This is where a contract spends most of its life.
- **Up for renewal** — Approaching the end of its term, where the customer decides whether to continue. See [renewal](/get-started/glossary).
- **Renewed** — Continued into a new term. If the customer increased their commitment, that's [expansion](/get-started/glossary); if they reduced it, [contraction](/get-started/glossary).
- **Churned** — Ended without renewing. The [ARR](/get-started/glossary) leaves your base.

A contract is always in exactly one stage. There's no in-between, and no contract sitting outside the model.

## Why stage, not activity

A CRM tracks activity: who emailed whom, which task is open, what the rep logged. That tells you what people are *doing*, but not what a contract *is* right now. HarborOS tracks the stage directly, because the stage is what your numbers depend on. A contract that's up for renewal counts differently than one that's already churned, no matter how much activity is attached to either.

This is what lets the rest of the product stay consistent. When a contract changes stage, every number that depends on it — recurring revenue, your renewal base, retention — moves with it automatically.

## How stage drives your numbers

Each stage feeds a different part of HarborOS:

- New and renewed contracts build your active [ARR](/get-started/glossary).
- Up-for-renewal contracts form your renewal base and show up in Renewals.
- Expansion and contraction at renewal are what separate [gross retention from net retention](/get-started/glossary).
- Churned contracts drop out of the base and appear as losses in your [ARR waterfall](/get-started/glossary).

Because the stage is tracked on the contract itself, these aren't separate counts you maintain — they're the same contracts, read through [different lenses](/concepts/lenses).
