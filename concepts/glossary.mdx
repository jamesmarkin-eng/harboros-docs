Every term HarborOS uses, defined in one place. Standard finance terms come first, then the ones specific to HarborOS. Other pages link back here on first use.

## Finance terms

**Annual Recurring Revenue (ARR)** — The annualized value of a customer's recurring contract: what they pay you per year for the recurring portion, regardless of how often they're billed. In HarborOS, ARR is calculated from your contracts rather than tracked separately.

**ARR waterfall (ARR movement)** — The breakdown of how your ARR changed from one period to the next, split into new business, expansion, contraction, and churn. Sometimes called the bridge.

**Churn** — Recurring revenue lost when a customer cancels or doesn't renew. Measured as the ARR that left.

**Contraction** — An existing customer reducing their contract — fewer seats, a lower tier, a smaller commitment — without leaving entirely. The opposite of expansion.

**Expansion** — Additional recurring revenue from an existing customer, such as upsell, more seats, or a higher tier, during the term or at renewal.

**Gross Revenue Retention (GRR)** — The share of recurring revenue you keep from your existing customers over a period, counting churn and contraction but not expansion. GRR can't exceed 100%.

**Net Revenue Retention (NRR)** — The same measure as GRR, but including expansion. A business with strong upsell can exceed 100%.

**Renewal** — The point where a contract term ends and the customer decides whether to continue. HarborOS treats it as a step in the [contract lifecycle](/concepts/lifecycle-model).

**Variance** — The difference between two versions of a number — for example, this week's forecast against last week's — along with the explanation of what caused the change.

## HarborOS terms

**Beacon** — HarborOS's built-in assistant. It answers questions and explains what sits behind a number by pointing to the contracts and records that produced it. See [Beacon](/concepts/beacon).

**Compass** — Your home base in HarborOS: the screen you start from to see where the business stands before moving into the detail.

**Contract** — The core record everything in HarborOS is built from. A contract holds the terms of a customer agreement, and every number in the product is derived from it. See [Contract is the atom](/concepts/contract-is-the-atom).

**Lens** — A way of describing how HarborOS's screens relate to each other. Pipeline, Renewals, Compass, Portolan, and Reports aren't separate tools; each is a different view, or lens, on the same contracts. See [Lenses](/concepts/lenses).

**Portolan** — The part of HarborOS that saves your forecast and your standing at a point in time, so you can look back at what you believed and when. It produces snapshots and positions. See [Portolan](/concepts/portolan).

**Position** — A committed version of where the business stands: what you believed at a specific moment and the assumptions behind it. Saved in Portolan.

**Snapshot** — A point-in-time capture of your numbers — forecast, renewal base, or pipeline — that you can compare against later to see what moved. Saved in Portolan.
