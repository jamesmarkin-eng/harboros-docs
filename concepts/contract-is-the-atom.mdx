Everything in HarborOS is built from one thing: the contract. When you add or change a contract, every number that relies on it updates with it. You don't keep your recurring revenue in one spreadsheet, your renewals in another, and a board summary in a third — and you never have to make them match, because they all come from the same contracts.

## What "the atom" means

An atom is the smallest building block — the thing everything else is made of. In HarborOS, that's the contract. It holds the facts once, and every screen and report is built on top of it.

In most companies it works the other way around. The numbers that matter — what you're earning, what's renewing, what you might lose — each live in their own spreadsheet that someone updates by hand. When a deal changes, you have to remember every place that number shows up and fix each one. HarborOS removes that step. You change the contract; everything downstream follows.

<Note>
Change a contract once, and every number that uses it updates with it. There's nothing to match up by hand.
</Note>

## What your contracts power

Because everything starts from the contract, your contracts feed the numbers you already track. A few you'll recognize:

- Recurring revenue
- What's coming up for renewal, and what looks at risk
- Growth and shrinkage within your existing customers
- What you've lost to churn
- How much revenue you're keeping (retention)
- How your revenue changed since last quarter
- Your forecast

This isn't the full list — and that's the point. Just about anything you'd normally pull together in a finance spreadsheet is built from the same contracts. So the numbers always agree with each other, and you can always see exactly which contracts produced them.

## Why it matters to you

When every number traces back to a contract, you can always answer "where did this come from?" — whether you're in a board meeting, talking to an investor, or just double-checking your own work. It's also why the different parts of HarborOS aren't separate tools: they're [different views of the same contracts](/concepts/lenses).
