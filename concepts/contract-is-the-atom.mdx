In HarborOS, the contract is the atom: the single canonical record from which every number derives. ARR, renewals, churn, expansion, retention, the board waterfall, the forecast — none of them are stored, maintained, or reconciled on their own. Each is a question asked of the same contracts.

## Why this matters

Most finance teams keep ARR in one place, the renewal forecast in another, and the board waterfall in a third, then reconcile them by hand every cycle. That spreadsheet in the middle isn't a discipline problem or a tooling problem — it's an [architecture problem](/get-started/what-is-harboros). The number has no home, so it lives wherever someone last built it.

A CRM tracks intent. An ERP tracks history. Neither models the contract's life — what renews, what expands, what's at risk. HarborOS does. Once the contract is the source of record, the numbers can't disagree, because they are all reading the same thing.

<Note>
One record, many views. Zero reconciliation.
</Note>

## What derives from the contract

| Number | The question it answers of the contracts |
| --- | --- |
| ARR | What recurring value is currently active? |
| Renewals | What is up for renewal, and what is at risk? |
| Expansion / contraction | How is the existing base growing or shrinking? |
| Churn | What has been lost? |
| GRR / NRR | How much revenue are we retaining, with and without expansion? |
| Waterfall | How did ARR move from one period to the next? |
| Forecast | What do we expect, and on what basis? |

Each is computed deterministically from the contract record — never entered by hand, never reconciled against a parallel copy.

## The consequence

Because every number traces back to a contract, every number is explainable and reconstructable — it survives board scrutiny and diligence. This is also why the product's surfaces are [lenses, not separate tools](/concepts/lenses): they look at one underlying reality from different angles. It is why [Portolan](/concepts/portolan) can preserve not just a number but the belief behind it, and why [Beacon](/concepts/beacon) can explain a movement by pointing at the contracts that caused it.

CRM tracks intent. ERP tracks history. HarborOS tracks truth.
